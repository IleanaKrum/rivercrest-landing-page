# Design Brainstorm: Rivercrest FMC Landing Page

## Context
Rivercrest Free Methodist Church is a faith-based community organization in Wichita, KS. Their Facebook presence emphasizes spiritual messaging, community, prayer, and unity. The brand uses deep navy/teal blues with green accents, professional photography, and inspirational biblical content.

---

## Design Approach 1: Modern Spiritual Minimalism
**Design Movement:** Contemporary Spiritual Design with Brutalist Clarity

**Core Principles:**
- Generous whitespace with intentional typography hierarchy
- Asymmetrical layouts that guide the eye through content
- Subtle spiritual symbolism integrated into design (not heavy-handed)
- Accessibility-first approach with clear visual hierarchy

**Color Philosophy:**
Primary palette centers on the church's existing navy/teal (#1a4d5c) as a grounding color representing stability and faith. Paired with warm cream (#faf8f5) for approachability and light gray (#4a5568) for text. Green accent (#2d7a6e) from the logo provides warmth and growth symbolism. This creates a sophisticated, welcoming atmosphere that feels both professional and spiritually grounded.

**Layout Paradigm:**
Asymmetrical grid with large hero image on one side, text content flowing on the other. Service cards arranged in a staggered, organic pattern rather than uniform rows. Content flows vertically with breathing room between sections.

**Signature Elements:**
- Subtle diagonal dividers between sections (representing forward movement)
- Hand-crafted serif typography for headings (representing tradition)
- Layered depth with soft shadows and card-based components
- Integrated spiritual imagery (light rays, open hands, unity circles)

**Interaction Philosophy:**
Smooth, purposeful transitions. Hover states that reveal additional information without overwhelming. Buttons that feel inviting rather than aggressive. Scroll-triggered subtle animations that enhance rather than distract.

**Animation:**
- Gentle fade-in on scroll for content blocks
- Smooth color transitions on hover (navy to green)
- Subtle scale-up on card hover (1.02x)
- Staggered entrance for service cards (100ms delay between each)

**Typography System:**
- Display: Crimson Text (serif) for headings—traditional, elegant, faith-forward
- Body: Inter (sans-serif) for readability and modern feel
- Hierarchy: 48px/40px (display), 24px (subheading), 16px (body), 14px (meta)

**Probability:** 0.08

## Design Approach 2: Warm Community-Focused Design
**Design Movement:** Contemporary Humanist Design with Organic Forms

**Core Principles:**
- Emphasis on human connection and community warmth
- Organic, flowing shapes and curves (no hard angles)
- Photography-forward design celebrating people and moments
- Inclusive and welcoming visual language

**Color Philosophy:**
Warm earth tones paired with the church's navy. Primary background is a soft warm white (#fffbf7). Navy (#1a4d5c) anchors the design with authority. Warm terracotta (#c97a5f) adds approachability and human warmth. Soft sage green (#a8b8a8) provides calm spirituality. This palette feels like a welcoming home rather than a corporate institution.

**Layout Paradigm:**
Full-width hero with large, warm photography. Content flows in organic, curved sections with overlapping elements. Service cards arranged in a flowing, non-linear pattern. Sidebar elements with testimonials and community stories.

**Signature Elements:**
- Rounded, organic shapes and soft corners throughout
- Large, authentic community photography
- Hand-drawn accent illustrations (prayer hands, hearts, community circles)
- Testimonial cards with profile photos and genuine quotes

**Interaction Philosophy:**
Warm, inviting interactions. Buttons feel like extended hands. Hover states that feel like gentle touches. Micro-interactions that celebrate user engagement.

**Animation:**
- Smooth parallax on hero image
- Cards that gently float on hover
- Testimonials that slide in from sides on scroll
- Pulse animation on call-to-action buttons

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display (serif) for headings—elegant and warm
- Body: Poppins (sans-serif) for friendly, approachable feel
- Hierarchy: 52px/44px (display), 28px (subheading), 16px (body), 13px (meta)

**Probability:** 0.07

## Design Approach 3: Bold Spiritual Statement Design
**Design Movement:** Contemporary Sacred Geometry with Intentional Contrast

**Core Principles:**
- Strong visual statements that command attention
- Sacred geometry and spiritual symbolism integrated throughout
- High contrast between elements for clarity and impact
- Purposeful use of color to convey spiritual meaning

**Color Philosophy:**
Deep navy (#1a4d5c) as dominant color representing the divine and stability. Paired with bright, energetic gold (#d4a574) for spiritual light and hope. Crisp white (#ffffff) for clarity and purity. Dark charcoal (#2d3748) for text. This creates a bold, spiritually intentional palette that feels reverent yet modern.

**Layout Paradigm:**
Hero section with bold typography overlaid on spiritual imagery. Content organized in distinct sections with clear visual separation. Service cards in a structured grid with geometric accents. Sidebar with key information and calls-to-action.

**Signature Elements:**
- Geometric patterns inspired by sacred geometry (circles, spirals, mandalas)
- Bold, oversized typography that makes statements
- Strategic use of gold accents for spiritual significance
- Integrated spiritual symbols (light rays, open doors, ascending paths)

**Interaction Philosophy:**
Purposeful, intentional interactions. Buttons feel like important decisions. Hover states that reveal deeper meaning. Animations that feel spiritual and meaningful.

**Animation:**
- Geometric patterns that animate on page load
- Gold accents that glow on hover
- Content blocks that slide in with purpose
- Rotating spiritual symbols in background

**Typography System:**
- Display: Abril Fatface (serif) for headings—bold, spiritual, commanding
- Body: Source Sans Pro (sans-serif) for clarity and accessibility
- Hierarchy: 56px/48px (display), 32px (subheading), 16px (body), 14px (meta)

**Probability:** 0.06

---

## Selected Design Approach: Modern Spiritual Minimalism

I'm moving forward with **Design Approach 1: Modern Spiritual Minimalism** because it best balances the church's professional identity with their spiritual mission. This approach:

- Respects the existing brand colors while elevating their presentation
- Creates breathing room for content without overwhelming visitors
- Uses asymmetrical layouts to feel contemporary and intentional
- Incorporates subtle spiritual elements without being heavy-handed
- Prioritizes accessibility and readability for a diverse congregation
- Allows photography and testimonials to shine as the primary storytellers

This design will feel welcoming to both longtime members and first-time visitors, while maintaining the professional credibility expected of a faith organization.
